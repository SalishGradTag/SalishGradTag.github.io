All decisions will be made according to these rules. Make sure to read them so that you know what to expect.

Tagging Rules and Process

DO NOT BREAK ANY LAWS

You must be granted permission to enter ANY private property


Safe Locations, Times, and Situations where you CANNOT be tagged:



- After the final 20 players remain, classes will no longer be off limits with individual teacher permission.

- Inside classrooms during a scheduled class

- Doing classwork outside of class during class time e.g. Film Production, Video Production, or Photography
*If you are outside of class and working you cannot tag others

- School field trips

- During, 10 minutes before, and 10 minutes after athletic practices and games (these 10 minutes give coaches a chance to talk)

- During, 15 minutes before, and 15 minutes after work/a shift

- The bathroom, if target is there to use stalls or urinals (do not be creepy)



Legal Locations, Times, and Situations where a target CAN be tagged:

- The hallway to and from classes

- The bathroom if target is not using the facilities (again, whether or not a target is legally allowed to be tagged should be clear, especially if they have a pen in their hand)

- Spares/Flex blocks even if the target is working

- Private property such as homes and gymnasiums with permission



Successfully tagging a target:

Tagger will be sent the target of the target they just tagged.
The tagged player is eliminated from the game.
This process continues until one player remains.



Game Rules:

Tie-Breakers (if there is more than one player left at the end):

- First tie-breaker: The player with the most successful tags wins.

- Second tie-breaker: If tags are equal, the player with the most recent successful tag wins.

Missing Class:

If a tagger reports that their target misses 5 consecutive days of school, it will be verified using attendance, and they will subsequently be eliminated

Target Each player starts with one assigned target. Players are only allowed to tag their specific target at any given time. At one point

Tagging Rules:

A tag is only valid if the tagger physically taps their target with their open hand. No objects, tools, or projectiles can be used to tag.

Confirming tags:

All tags must be recorded on video to be considered valid.

The video must clearly show:

- The tag occurring.

- The tagged player's face.

The video must be sent to the official grad tag instagram account within 5 hours of the tag.

Tagged Players:

Once tagged, players are eliminated and can no longer tag others. Their target is passed on to their tagger.

Leaderboard:

Tiebreakers for players still in the game are decided by:

- Number of tags

- Most recent tag

Tiebreakers for players who are out of the game are decided by:

- Number of tags

- Time survived in the game
