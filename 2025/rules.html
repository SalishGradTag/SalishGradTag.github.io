Tagging Rules and Process

DO NOT BREAK ANY LAWS

You must be granted permission to enter private property

To participate, you must be currently enrolled in one class at Salish



All decisions will be made according to these rules. Make sure to read them so that you know what to expect.


Tagging Rules:

A tag is only valid if the tagger physically taps their target with their open hand. No objects, tools, or projectiles can be used to tag unless the immunity item rules at the time create an exception.

All tags must be recorded on video to be considered valid.

The video must show:

- The tag occurring.

- The tagged player's face.

- Proof for any rules that might apply (immunity items, cars not moving, the time)

The video must be sent to the official grad tag instagram account within 6 hours of the tag.

- Each week either have assigned target(s) or will not.

- Your target(s) will be sent to you through teams.

- When targets are assigned you may only tag people who are your target.

- When targets are not assigned you may tag anyone.

- The tagged player is eliminated from the game.


Leaderboard:

The leaderboard is ranked in the following order:

- Being alive

- Number of tags

- Recency of their last tag or the time survived

Prizes will be announced soon.


Safe situations where you CANNOT be tagged:

- Until only 20 players remain or May 12, whichever comes first, you will be safe when in class.

- You are in class if:

> Inside classrooms during a scheduled class

> Doing classwork outside of class (e.g. Film Production, Video Production, Photography, or Band). You may not tag people while doing class work outside of classrorom. This is to prevent people from tagging others while not taking any risk themselves.

> School field trips

> During, 10 minutes before, and 10 minutes after, band practices & concerts, and athletic practices & games (these 10 minutes give coaches and such a chance to talk)

> During, 10 minutes before, and 10 minutes after work/a shift

> Cars that are either moving or on the road.


Situations where you CAN be tagged:

- Any situation not outlined above

- While switching classes

- Going to water fountain or the bathroom (Don't be weird)

- Spares/Flex blocks even if the target is doing work


Immunity

- Each date will have one immunity item

- The immunity item will be announced for a range of dates.

- The rules for each immunity item will be defined once it is announced. Be sure to check the rules to make sure you are in fitting the criteria for each week.

- Any person following the immunity item criteria will be immune from getting tagged

- Purges may have exceptionally difficult items to obtain


Exceptional Circumstances

If you report that your target that has been missing for 5 consecutive days of school they will be eliminated after their attendance is verified. You will not gain a tag from this but will get a new target.

Other exceptional circumstances will be decided on by the grad tag team.
